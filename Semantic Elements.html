Importance of Exercises in Teenage

Exercise and sports are essential for teens as they promote physical fitness, improve cardiovascular health, and help in maintaining a healthy weight. Regular physical activity enhances muscle strength, flexibility, and endurance while reducing the risk of chronic diseases. It also plays a key role in mental well-being by relieving stress, boosting self-esteem, and improving focus and academic performance. Moreover, sports foster teamwork, discipline, and social skills, making them crucial for overall development during adolescence. this.

These exercises require no equipment and are great for improving overall fitness.

Jumping Jacks: Stand straight and jump while spreading your arms and legs out, then return to the starting position. This is great for warming up and cardio.

Squats: Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, lower your body as if sitting in a chair, then stand back up. Squats help strengthen your legs and core.

Lunges: Step forward with one leg, bend both knees until your back knee nearly touches the floor, and push back up. Repeat with the other leg. Lunges are excellent for leg muscles.

Push-ups: Start in a plank position, then lower your body until your chest is just above the ground, and push back up. They work your chest, arms, and core.

Arm Circles: Stand with your arms extended at shoulder height and make small circles with your arms, gradually increasing the size. This strengthens shoulder muscles.

Plank: Hold a push-up position with your body straight and supported by your hands or forearms and toes. This strengthens the core and improves posture.

Leg Raises: Lie on your back, raise your legs to a 90-degree angle, and then lower them slowly without touching the ground. This targets the lower abdominal muscles.

High Knees: Jog in place while bringing your knees as high as possible. It helps with cardio and leg strength.
